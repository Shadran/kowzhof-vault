The Keeper

You awake from your slumber.

This time, it's not the ringing of a bell to wake you up, but the clamor of people. Sounds of furniture being moved, cutlery being laid out, fires roaring and rushed steps to and from the castle echo in those usually silent courtyards.

Several hooded people, two dozens or maybe more, are intent in preparing the dining hall. Lit torches hang on the walls, bottles of wine and casks of ale have been placed around and a beautiful smell of roasted meats comes from the kitchen. It would be a pretty festive sight if it wasn't for all those black-shrouded figures working in unison, not a single word being exchanged between them.

As you approach the hall attracted by all the ruckus, you get met by the usual voice of The Keeper. This time he is not standing atop the steps leading to the throne, but right in the middle of the hall.

"With a banquet! This is how we start this glorious final day in its celebration! The Ritual proceeds, one more has been sacrificed, and now we approach the end of our ceremony!" As he pauses, all the hooded cultists within earshot turn towards him and bow their head. The room gets awfully quiet for a couple seconds, before everyone returns to their previous task.

"Feast, you all!" he then continues, motioning with his arms at the lot of you. "Feast so that your soul and body may be filled, so that the last two sacrifices of tonight may bask in his abhorrent glory!"

Done with his speech, he returns to directing the preparations for the banquet. He hits and prods and berates the poor figures every time something is not to his liking, and you get the feeling that whatever food is being prepared won't start coming out until midday.

The Ritual is almost over.

The Keeper

The Banquet

After much shuffling of plates and tables, all preparations seem to be done. The Dining Hall shines with the light of a hundred candle flames, glowing bright atop golden chandeliers. Lanterns hang on the walls, providing a comforting orange glow.

As the sun starts to set and disappear behind the walls of the castle, the servants of the Wretched One bring out a multitude of trays with enough food to feed a legion.

Massive bowls are placed at the ends of tables, filled with a rainbow assortment of juicy fruits both familiar and unknown. Violet grapes drape off the sides, resting atop big spiraling shapes with a yellow texture akin to lemon zest. Big red berries, almost transparent and watery, peek from behind strange apples with a patchwork of bright colors on their skin.

Fish is brought out next, served on sharp skewers with an incredible assortment of roasted seafood. Species you cannot recognize, both big and small, lined up in a mouth-watering spectacle. Not only fish but crustaceans with three or four claws, jelly-like assortments of tentacles, shrimps as big as a fist, and more.

Lastly, the meat. With great ceremony, four attendants bring forth a massive tray. On top of it, ribs of an unspecified beast, grilled and browned to perfection. As long as an arm, they look like a single one of them could feed you for a day.

Casks of ale and wine immediately follow. They get uncorked, and the contents start flowing freely, with bowls immediately placed under to catch the pouring liquid. As the liquids fall inside, they vanish, and the flow never stops. It seems you can serve yourself by just bringing a cup to these never-ending streams.

"My dear guests! The Wretched One provides!" When all the food has been brought, the Keeper advances with ceremony. "Here is a banquet, to nourish your bodies and your spirits alike! It is content with your actions, and us humble servants have prepared this meal for you! Let us all eat and drink in its name!"
