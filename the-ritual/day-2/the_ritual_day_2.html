The Keeper

Pale light timidly filters from beyond the castle walls, choked by dark clouds threatening rain.

It is morning.

The rhythmical clamor of a bell calls for everyone to gather, and as you rise from your torpor and move towards the sound you notice the Throne Room doors are wide open and a hooded figure is already standing behind the bloodied throne.

After you are all gathered, the Keeper steps in the open. Much like the previous day, he raises a hand to prepare for an announcement, resting the other on the slimy chunks of organic matter surrounding him. The hall quietens.

“Servants of the Wretched One! You have done well! That hungry, curious lady is now feasting in his turpid halls and the doll as well! The Ritual proceeds as planned. Although it’s unfortunate that one of the sacrifices willingly decided to unravel themselves and deprived you all of the pleasure of becoming executioners for a second time, I am more than sure that you will make up for it soon.” A wicked laugh echoes through the hall, the hunchback clearly satisfied with the events that transpired during the night.

“We are now entering the second day of this Ritual. All is ready for you to prove once more your desire to serve it! Since you have done well, today you will have again to sacrifice just one person. Who will it be? Who will it be?!”

Taking a few excited steps, the man descends from his vantage point and passes by you all. His introduction done, he stares with bulging eyes as he takes his leave. This time there is no flashy disappearance: he opens the door to his right and vanishes down a corridor, presumably to his usual spot in the Warden’s Room.
